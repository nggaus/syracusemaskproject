Hand sewn masks are needed in central New York


You've made masks for your family and friends.   Keep sewing!

With the recent appeal by Gov. Cuomo that all New Yorkers wear masks in close public areas, many government services are now in need of fabric masks for their essential employees who work with the public. We still desperately need sewists to fill those requests. Check the Drop Off Sites page for a location near you!

Why this website?

As you have likely heard, there is a national shortage of surgical masks. Hospitals are in desperate need. They are beginning to take hand sewn masks as a stop gap until the shortages are alleviated.

There are plenty of patterns and advice online, and a lot of people jumping in to make masks, but it wasn’t clear to me that there was any organized effort to support hospitals in central New York. I wasn’t willing to proceed until I knew that 1) what I was making was acceptable; 2) I knew where they were needed locally; and 3) I knew how to deliver them without creating more confusion at busy hospitals and medical facilities.

I finally got answers. A hospital committee at SUNY Upstate looked into the materials and design needed to make hand sewn masks as safe as possible for their healthcare workers to use during a shortage of CDC-approved N95 masks. When they released the design, I created this webpage. That design, and others, are posted on the DESIGNS page along with sewing tips, clarifications, pictures, and videos to make it easier for local sewists to produce masks acceptable for healthcare professionals.

If you are sewing for hospitals and health centers, the Open-Source COVID19 Situation and Supply Needs (OSCMS) group emphasizes that you should be working with a group that is using a vetted design and has a distribution network setup so as not to overwhelm the hospitals. Don't reinvent the wheel and go it alone unless there is nothing set up in your area. Find a sewing group in central New York.

DISCLOSURE:
The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention does not endorse fabric masks as a sole preventive measure to shield healthcare workers from the coronavirus. Due to the extreme shortage of PPE in the United States, however, hospitals are requesting hand sewn masks to wear over top of standard CDC-approved N95 masks in order to extend the life of the approved masks. Additionally, some healthcare faciities are running out of N95 masks and are using hand sewn fabric masks in liu of wearing no face protection at all. I have listed several hospitals on my Drop Off Sites page which are asking for hand sewn masks to extend or supplement their dwindling supplies. I have made every effort to include links to the instructions these hospitals have released for masks that they will accept. See the Designs and Sewing Groups pages for more information on locally requested mask styles.
